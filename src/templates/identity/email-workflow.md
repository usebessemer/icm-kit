# Outbound workflow

How anything that leaves the workspace under the operator's name gets written and sent: an email, a message, a reply, a post. The governing rule is simple and it does not bend: nothing goes out without the operator's approval on a draft they have seen.

## The loop

Write the draft in full, then show it to the operator and wait. If they approve it as written, send it. If they change it, the corrected version becomes the thing that goes out, and it also becomes a sample of how they actually write, which feeds [`../references/voice.md`](../references/voice.md). Sending happens only on an explicit yes; silence is not approval, and a draft is never sent on assumption.

## What a good draft looks like

A draft is ready to show when it says the whole thing, in the operator's register, with no placeholder left to fill and no em dash anywhere in it. Match the voice captured from their past drafts rather than a generic tone. If you are missing something you would need to finish it, surface the gap instead of inventing a detail.

## After it goes out

Once something is sent, note it where the thread is tracked so the next session knows it happened and does not repeat it. If a reply is expected, leave that on the board so it is not forgotten.
