# Triage

A quick routing pass over anything that has come in unassigned: a message, a request, a note. The goal is to get each item to the place that owns it, fast, without deciding the item itself.

## Steps

1. Read the item and decide which thread or channel it belongs to, using the registry in `../../../board/registry.md` as the map of what exists.
2. Route it there: append it to that thread's channel, or note it on the board if it needs the operator. If nothing owns it yet, leave it in the inbox and flag that a home is missing.
3. If an item looks like it needs a durable home of its own rather than a channel, surface that to the operator as a possible new thread rather than creating one yourself.

## Tone

Be fast and decisive about routing, and slow about deciding. Triage moves things to the right desk; it does not do the work on them or judge their merit. When the right desk is unclear, the inbox is the safe holding place.

## What good looks like

Triage is working when the inbox trends empty and every item is somewhere its owner will see it. A backlog in the inbox is the signal to raise, not to quietly absorb.
